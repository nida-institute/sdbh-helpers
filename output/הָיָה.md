## Observations

- The highest frequency of הָיָה with human subjects indicates its role in personal and communal identity within narratives.
- The significant use with abstract subjects highlights the verb's function in expressing states and conditions.
- The divine occurrences underscore the verb's theological significance in conveying God's presence and actions.
- The implicit subject instances reveal the Hebrew language's characteristic of omitting subjects when contextually understood.

## Unresolved

- **Implicit (702 instances):** These clauses lack an explicit subject, often due to pro-drop syntax or pronouns understood from context.
- **Unknown (422 instances):** These involve indeterminate subjects, either due to contextual ambiguity or uncoded nouns, necessitating further study for clarity.
