## Observations

- The dominance of "People" as a subject type underscores the verb's role in narrating human actions and statuses.
- The high frequency of implicit subjects reflects the Hebrew language's pro-drop nature, where context often determines subject identity.
- Abstract subjects highlight the verb's utility in expressing intangible states or qualities, such as emotions or conditions.
- The significant presence of divine subjects points to theological themes, where "הָיָה" often conveys divine presence or action.
